About Us

This Grievance and Redressal Portal is a dedicated initiative developed to ensure a fair, transparent, and timely resolution process for all members of our college community. Our team is committed to upholding these values through a platform that is accessible and efficient for both students and staff.

Our Mission

Our mission is to foster a safe, positive, and inclusive college environment by providing a secure and confidential platform for voicing concerns. We strive to address every grievance with empathy, integrity, and a steadfast commitment to a constructive resolution.

About Our College

Our History

Founded in 1960 at Elphinstone Technical School, Government Polytechnic, Mumbai (GPM) is one of the oldest and most prestigious diploma engineering colleges in Maharashtra. This institute relocated to its present campus in Bandra in 1985 and was awarded academic autonomy in 1990. For decades, GPM has been a cornerstone of higher technical education, pioneering capacity and quality improvements through significant initiatives, including the World Bank Assisted Projects (1990â€“1998) and the Canada India Industry Polytechnic Linkage Project (2002).

Vision & Values

Our vision is guided by our motto: "Transform Knowledge into Work." We strive to be a leading academically autonomous institute, committed to developing competent, industry-ready technicians and fostering sustainable development through continuous improvement in our curriculum. We maintain strong liaison with industries and implement an outcome-based curriculum that includes mandatory six-month in-plant training for all students.

Core Values:

  • Excellence: Upholding high academic standards as an autonomous, government-supported body.
  • Innovation: Implementing modern curricula and encouraging project-based learning using the latest tools and technologies.
  • Industry-Readiness: Focusing on practical skills and hands-on experience through the sandwich-pattern diploma programs.
  • Inclusivity: Dedicated to community development and bringing Persons With Disabilities (PWD) into the mainstream of technical education.
  • Autonomy: Utilizing self-governance to continuously adapt and keep pace with emerging technologies.
